One of the patches aren't fixes for a compile error, but there was some optimisations the compiler couldn't do without the patch.
It's an instruction reorder.
It's tested with parts of zlib, where it measured the number of points it could try for a reorder, and it went from 196 to ~450, so it went up a lot.
It may not always result in better code, but should on average make some impact..
The other 3 are just errors.
The dropping of the volatile may be important, maybe not.
It can rearrange writing to IO registers, which may lead to hardware doing strange things.
